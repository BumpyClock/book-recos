# Reading Profile

## Core Identity
- **Sci-fi backbone, actively broadening** — sci-fi is home base but actively seeking more literary fiction and diverse genres
- **Quality-driven** — goes where strong recommendations lead; genre doesn't drive the choice, quality does
- **Series lover** — prefers series to binge, the longer the better
- **Wit matters** — a funny, distinctive narrative voice can make or break a book
- Reads on **Kindle/ebook and physical books** (no audiobooks)

## Sci-Fi Preferences (Primary Genre)
- Loves ALL dimensions: characters, world-building, plot/pacing, and big ideas
- Emotional depth and character building are key — not just spectacle
- **Literary quality > raw thriller pacing** — prefers better writing, tighter plots, and deeper characters over pure action (Alien Clay > Parasitology)
- **Moral complexity and worldview-deconstruction** — loves protagonists who start wrong and evolve (Some Desperate Glory)
- **Cozy/hopeful sci-fi** (Wayfarers) actively sought out alongside harder stuff (Expanse, Three-Body)
- **Mystery + speculative fiction mashups** are a sweet spot (Tainted Cup, Drop of Corruption)
- **Meta-humor works** but the premise must earn its keep; derivative setups are a drag even in comedy (Redshirts: fun but could be better)
- Can handle **dark/bleak** tones if the writing is good (The Road, World War Z)
- **Murderbot loyalist** — the voice never gets old across 7 books; consistent 5★
- 5-star sci-fi: Expanse series, Murderbot series, Bobiverse, Wayfarers, Three-Body Problem, The Martian, Ender's Game, Ministry of Time, Tainted Cup/Drop of Corruption, Alien Clay, Some Desperate Glory

## Literary Fiction Preferences
- Loves **multi-generational sweeping epics** (Covenant of Water)
- Values **satirical/prophetic voice** (Super Sad True Love Story = possibly favorite book ever — loved the satire, love story, Lenny's vulnerability, and the writing style)
- Enjoys **genre-bending literary fiction** (Ministry of Time — mashup + immigrant identity + humor)
- Identity & belonging themes resonate
- Other 5-stars: Room, Lover's Dictionary, Kane & Abel

## Fantasy
- Still wants **adult/epic fantasy** — has outgrown YA dystopia
- Enjoyed Game of Thrones, Clash of Kings (5 stars both)

## Non-Fiction Interests
- **History & politics** — especially Indian history, geopolitics, American politics
- **Science & ideas** — popular science, philosophy, behavioral psychology
- **Business & career** — leadership, productivity, entrepreneurship
- 5-stars: Justice (Sandel), Obama's Wars, A Higher Loyalty, India Calling, Orbiting the Giant Hairball

## South Asian Connection
- **Actively wants more South Asian literature and authors**
- Has read: Panipat, Sacred Games (to-read), India Calling, Inscrutable Americans, Inglorious Empire (to-read), Covenant of Water, God of Small Things (to-read)

## Humor & Memoir
- Enjoys funny memoirs/essay collections: Sh*t My Dad Says (5★), Man Made (5★), Bossypants, I Am America
- Wit in narrative voice is "very important" across all genres

## Dealbreakers
1. **Flat characters** — no cardboard protagonists
2. **Derivative plots** — must add something new
3. **No stakes/tension** — characters surviving everything kills engagement
4. **Bad/cringeworthy dialogue**
5. **Sexist dialogue or bigotry**

## Taste Evolution
- Early reads (2010-2012): YA dystopia, Dan Brown, classic thrillers
- Current taste: more sophisticated sci-fi, literary fiction, genre mashups, character-driven stories
- Has matured past YA but still enjoys epic fantasy
